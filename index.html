Our Story

Side Note: While this story is written from Ashley’s perspective, it was written by both of us in our living room while snuggling with our adorable cats, Jasper and Friday.

Owen and I can’t remember exactly when we met. Having gone to middle school together and having both been members of the band, we can only assume we must have met then, me with my little clarinet and Owen with his giant tuba. However, honestly we don’t really remember our first meeting. Maybe it was band, maybe our sophomore year science class, but our first clear memories of each other came in the spring of 2004 when, through our mutual friends, Claire and Preston, we started hanging out.

The four of us had a lot of fun that summer, going to the roller rink, miniature golfing and, of course, playing epic games of Apples to Apples. Sometime that summer Owen showed me the collection of music on his I-Pod, and I said to myself, “Simply based off his music taste, I could marry this guy!” I had no idea how right that statement would end up being. However, after that summer Owen headed to college in Colorado, and I stayed in Ashland to attend Southern Oregon University.

Over the years we stayed in touch. We wrote letters back and forth while Owen was working at a summer camp, we talked during the occasional AIM chat and always met up at Christmas time when he was back in town. In 2009, we were both home for the holidays, which meant getting to spend some time together. We enjoyed Ashland’s beautiful Christmas lights, saw each other out with friends and joined in the traditional King family cookie party. Two months later, I decided to visit my friend Therese who had recently moved to Denver. While there, I went and visited Owen in Gunnison, Colorado.

He picked me up at the bus stop in Salida, an hour away from Gunnison, and we drove over beautiful Monarch Pass into the amazing Gunnison Valley. That night we went to dinner at the Gunnysack in Gunnison. Looking back on this dinner now, we consider this as our first date. We spent the rest of the visit snowshoeing in the Black Canyon, skiing on Monarch Mountain and exploring Gunnison. In these three days, we began to fall for each other. We realized the deep connection we had with one another which had sprouted from our long friendship and appreciation for each others’ unique qualities. A month after our Colorado adventure, we decided that I should move to Colorado to see if we could make the relationship work. Well we made it work, and we have been together having a blast ever since. Some people ask us if we are marrying our high school sweethearts, to which we say, “No, but I am marrying my best friend.”

The Proposal

June of 2013 was a particularly rainy month in Southern Oregon. At the time, Owen and I had one afternoon a week off together. We generally liked to go hiking or fishing together on these days. During this particular June, it rained every Monday we had off together. Owen seemed to be getting increasingly upset by this bad weather, because for some unknown reason we just had to hike to the top of Mt. Ashland. On the very last Monday of the month, it was once again raining. Owen was again annoyed, so I told him, “Let’s just hike anyways. We are Oregonians! We can handle the rain.” So we headed up to the mountain.

As we drove across the backside of Mt. Ashland, it began to rain even harder and started to mix with snow. We drove as close to top of the mountain as we could to avoid getting drenched. We bundled up and started our mad dash to the summit. It was raining so hard that I was completely soaked in a matter of minutes. The whole time I was thinking to myself, “And why did we have to do this hike today? It’s raining and freezing, and the hike is so short it’s hardly even worth it!” However, once we got to the summit, even I had to admit it was beautiful looking down at the Rogue Valley sitting below the thick blanket of fog.

You can see for miles from the top of Mt Ashland, and as I was admiring the view Owen pulled out a wrapped box. The box was small and flat, clearly not the size of a ring box. Owen told me it was a gift from a trip to Mexico we had taken the previous March. Confused as to why he hadn’t given it to me before, I opened the box to find a beautiful silver heart shaped locket. The locket folded out into a four-leaf clover. In each leaf of the clover was a word spelling out a question I had been hoping to hear: “Will You Marry Me?” I looked up to see Owen down on one knee. All of Owen’s strange behavior that day suddenly made sense, and I quickly shouted, “Yes!” He kissed me, took off my glove and placed the ring on my finger. We stayed there for a minute admiring the view, and Owen asked me, “Should we stay and savor the moment?” We both looked at each and said “No way, it’s freezing!” Thus, we started our engagement with a mad dash to the car, soaked to the bone but happy as could be!

Ashley and Owen
